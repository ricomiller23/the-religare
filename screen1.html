THE RELIGARE — How to Use This Tool
FRAMEWORK

The word Religare comes from Latin meaning 'to bind back' — the same root word as religion itself.

Just as a telescope does not have opinions about the stars it shows you, the Religare does not have opinions about the religions it reveals. It is a neutral framework for extracting functional data from any belief system — and placing that data into a standardized grid, like a nutritional label.

When you can read a nutritional label, you can pick up any food product, turn it sideways, and instantly understand what is inside. The Religare does the same for religion.

THE GOLDEN RULES:
  • ◆ We look at what a religion DOES, not what it CLAIMS
  • ◆ We never take a religion out of its TIME PERIOD
  • ◆ Religion is based on MYTH, not fact — we collect facts ABOUT myths
  • ◆ Culture shapes everything — trace origins, not current spread
  • ◆ Every religion came from another religion
  • ◆ Keep an open mind. 90% of what we know is wrong.
THE RELIGARE LABEL

Question: "When and where did it begin? Always keep it in its time."

Why: "A religion born in 600 BCE answers different problems than one born in 1614 CE. Context is everything."

Question: "Who started it? Was it one person? An institution? A fictional character?"

Why: "The mechanism of founding reveals the religion's purpose."

Question: "What is the primary written source? When was it compiled? By whom?"

Why: "The text is the religion's operating manual — and its editing history is as important as its content."

Question: "What specific culture gave birth to this religion? Remember: religions spread. Trace back to the origin."

Why: "Culture paints the vessel. The Religare strips the paint."

Question: "How does this religion explain how the universe came into being? What is the structure of reality?"

Why: "Every religion has an answer to this question. Comparing them is the fastest path to discernment."

Question: "What does it claim is true about existence, humanity, and the divine?"

Why: "This is the doctrine — the official position."

Question: "What did this religion DO for the people of its time? What specific problem did it solve?"

Why: "This is the Religare's most powerful lens. Strip away what it claims — what did it actually accomplish?"

Question: "Who holds authority? How is it organized? Who controls access to God?"

Why: "Power structure reveals who benefits from the religion."

Question: "Where did it came from? What did it absorb? What did it react against?"

Why: "No religion was born from nothing. All came from another."

Question: "What did it leave behind? Why did it matter then — and what survives now?"

Why: "The legacy is the religion's actual impact on history, stripped of its self-assessment."
