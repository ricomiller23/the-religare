LINEAR TIMELINE
History is a flat circle. Or perhaps a straight line.

The timeline view places all three traditions into absolute historical context, showing parallel developments and critical intersections.

~1500–600 BCE

Zoroaster Preaches

In ancient Persia, Zarathustra breaks with polytheism and introduces cosmic dualism, free will, and a single supreme creator (Ahura Mazda).

538 BCE

Cyrus Frees the Jews

Zoroastrian King Cyrus the Great of Persia destroys the Babylonian Empire and frees the captive Jewish people. Syncretization of Zoroastrian cosmology into Jewish thought accelerates.

~30 AD

Crucifixion of Jesus

A Jewish preacher in Roman-occupied Jerusalem is executed. His followers form a new sect claiming he is the long-awaited Messiah (a concept adapted from the Persian Saoshyant).

313 AD

Edict of Milan

Emperor Constantine legalizes Christianity in the Roman Empire, beginning the shift from a persecuted minority cult to an imperial power structure.

325 AD

Council of Nicaea

Constantine convenes Christian bishops to formalize doctrine and unify the empire. The Trinity is established. Institutional Catholicism is born.

651 AD

Fall of Sassanid Empire

Islamic conquest of Persia destroys the final Zoroastrian empire. Many practitioners flee to India, becoming the Parsi community.

1517 AD

Protestant Reformation

Martin Luther publishes his 95 Theses, shattering the monolithic authority of the Catholic Church in Western Europe.

1610 AD

Galileo's Telescope

Galileo publishes Sidereus Nuncius, proving Jupiter has moons and shattering the Church-endorsed geocentric model of the universe.

1614–1616 AD

The Rosicrucian Manifestos

Three anonymous pamphlets are published in Germany, synthesizing Christianity, alchemy, and science into a call for universal reformation.

1975 AD

Galileo Pardoned

The Catholic Church officially acknowledges Galileo was correct.
